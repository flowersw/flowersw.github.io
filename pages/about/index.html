About

I was introduced to programming when I was a Mathematics undergraduate student at UNC-Chapel Hill (~2015). At the time, my perception of the Python programming language was that it was interesting but mainly used in academia, and that it had already had it’s “heyday”. It wasn’t until I graduated, learned more, and started working as a programmer that I realized the immensity, and thriving community that the language had. Since then, I’ve continued to be in awe as I dive deeper and deeper into the rabbit hole (and subsequent rabbit holes) that is programming, software development and testing, and the many applications of it.

I’m active in my local and native community of Durham, NC through meetups and workshops, and I have a strong desire to give back.

  • I competed with the ballroom dance team at UNC up and down the East Coast.
  • I once ran through a glass pane without stopping, and came out unscathed.
